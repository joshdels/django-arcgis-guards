Since your goal is to **finish and monetize the security agency MVP**, I'd prioritize features based on what completes the agency's operational workflow rather than administrative conveniences.

Here's the order I'd build:

### 🥇 Phase 1 — Complete the Operations Workflow

1. **Guard Deployment & Assignment** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

   * Assign guards to contracts.
   * Show required vs. assigned guards.
   * Handle reassignment.
   * View deployments by contract or date.

   This is the feature that turns your system into an operations platform instead of just a CRUD app.

2. **Operations Dashboard** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   Display things like:

   * Active contracts
   * Required guards
   * Assigned guards
   * Vacant positions
   * Guards on leave
   * Expiring contracts

3. **Duty/Shift Scheduling** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
   Even a simple version:

   * Morning
   * Afternoon
   * Night

---

### 🥈 Phase 2 — Financial Workflow

Once deployments are working:

4. **Billing Configuration**
   At the contract level, define things like:

   ```
   Billing Type
   ○ Fixed Monthly
   ○ Per Guard
   ○ Per Shift
   ○ Custom
   ```

   And:

   ```
   Billing Cycle

   Monthly
   Semi-monthly
   Quarterly
   ```

5. **Invoice Generation**
   Since every contract already knows:

   * client
   * number of guards
   * billing type
   * billing cycle

   the system can generate invoices automatically.

Example:

```
ABC Mall

12 Guards

Rate:
₱18,000 / guard

Total:
₱216,000

Invoice
July 2026
```

---

### 🥉 Phase 3 — Nice Business Features

* Payments
* Outstanding balances
* Official receipts
* Collections dashboard
* Payroll integration (later)

---

## I wouldn't build invoices first.

An invoice answers:

> **"What should we bill?"**

But your system doesn't yet fully answer:

> **"Who actually worked under this contract?"**

That's why **deployment comes before billing**.

---
